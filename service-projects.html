A Tradition of Service

Every semester, our chapter completes a service project for the benefit of those in the community. We appreciate all that the community does that enables our chapter to exist, and love our opportunities to improve our community. Below are listed some organizations we work with in our service projects.

KaBOOM's Map of Play

An important part of any child’s development is safe and wholesome recreation. Playtime, especially in the outdoors, is a key factor in a child’s life. However, especially in unfamiliar neighborhoods, parents often have a hard time finding playgrounds that are local, safe and fun for their children. That is where KaBOOM’s Map of Play comes in. Relying on community involvement, this web service documents playgrounds and other similar locations such as parks and tracks. Through user reviews, locations get rated and more information becomes available. It’s a great program, but sadly doesn’t have nearly any involvement in our area. For our service projects we organized initiatives in the chapter to visit as many local playgrounds as we could, collecting information so we could improve the available information on the site. Through our efforts, parents in our community will now have easy access to information that will greatly assist them in choosing where their child plays.

Utah Food Bank

Even in this day and age, hunger and starvation are still real threats. For example, 1 in 5 kids in Utah are unsure where their next meal is coming from. The Utah Food Bank seeks to combat this hunger problem, but relies on volunteer work to accomplish this task. From running food drives to sorting collected food, at every level the Utah Food Bank relies on volunteer work. The Skyline TSA Chapter volunteered at the Utah Food Bank to aid in sorting collected food. We sent 12 members to this event, and it was a major success. Thanks to our efforts, we were able to help those less fortunate to know where their next meeting is coming from.
